# Veritas — demo video plan (target: 2:45, hard cap 3:00)

## Before you record — prep so you're not waiting on APIs on camera

Live generation (NVIDIA) can take 10-20s. Dead air on a demo video kills
pacing, so pre-generate a few assets *before* you start recording:

1. Generate 3-4 single images with strong, visual prompts (something that
   reads well at a glance — e.g. "a cinematic shot of a fox in a snowy
   forest at dusk" beats "test image 1"). Let a couple of them sit in the
   gallery already.
2. Run one campaign (4-5 variants) ahead of time, so the campaign tag and
   grouping are already visible in the gallery when you start recording.
3. Keep **one prompt un-generated** so you can demo the live "type a
   prompt → click Generate → it appears" moment for real, once, on camera.
   Pick something fast/simple so the wait is short.
4. Have one already-generated image file saved locally (downloaded from
   the gallery) to drag into `/verify` — and have one random unrelated
   image (a screenshot, a stock photo, anything never generated by
   Veritas) ready to drag in as the "not verified" contrast case.
5. Open a second terminal/tab logged into the Backblaze B2 console ahead
   of time, on the "Object Lock" settings of `veritas-genmedia-locked`, so
   you can cut to it instantly instead of navigating live.

Recording tool: Windows Game Bar (Win+G) is built in and fine for a
browser-only demo. OBS Studio (free) if you want to also show a terminal
window in the same shot. Edit/trim in Clipchamp (built into Windows) or
CapCut — you mainly just need to cut dead air and add captions.

Voice: record a clean voiceover script (below) either live while
screen-recording, or record video silent and narrate over it after — the
second is easier to get right since you're not performing and clicking at
the same time.

## Shot-by-shot script

### 1. Hook — the problem (0:00–0:12)

**Visual:** Black slide with bold white/yellow text, or just cold-open on
your face/voice over a black screen. No UI yet.

**VO:**
> "Every week there's a story about an AI image passed off as real — or a
> real one dismissed as fake. We got really good at generating media. We
> never got a way to prove where it came from."

### 2. Introduce Veritas (0:12–0:25)

**Visual:** Cut to the live homepage, https://veritas-one-alpha.vercel.app
— let the hero load, hold on the headline "EVERY ASSET, VERIFIED FOREVER."

**VO:**
> "This is Veritas. Every image, video, or audio file you generate here
> ships with a cryptographic provenance manifest — stored on Backblaze
> B2 — the moment it's born. Anyone can check it. Not just you."

### 3. Live generate (0:25–1:05)

**Visual:** Type your pre-picked short prompt into the console. Click
Generate. While it's processing, either let 5-8s play naturally (it's
short if you picked a fast prompt) or narrate straight through it. Cut to
the result appearing in the gallery with the yellow "VERIFIED" corner tag.
Click the card — the provenance modal opens. Scroll to show: sha-256,
provider/model, "stored at" B2 key. Click "Manifest" to expand the raw
JSON briefly.

**VO:**
> "I type a prompt, pick a modality, hit generate. Behind the scenes,
> Genblaze runs the actual generation — right now through NVIDIA's
> flux-point-one-dev model — uploads the result to B2, and produces a
> signed manifest. Genblaze calls `Manifest.verify()` before this app ever
> shows a 'verified' badge, so this isn't a status I set myself — it's a
> passed cryptographic check. Here's the sha-256, the exact model that
> made it, and precisely where it lives in B2."

### 4. Iteration lineage (1:05–1:20)

**Visual:** In the same modal, click "Regenerate (linked)". Once the new
version appears, open its modal and show the "Iteration lineage" list —
point at the chain of run IDs.

**VO:**
> "Regenerate, and the new version links back to the one it came from.
> Every edit is traceable — not just the latest state."

### 5. Campaign fan-out (1:20–1:50)

**Visual:** Switch to Campaign mode. Show the already-run campaign in the
gallery (grouped by the green "CAMPAIGN" tag) — or, if time allows, kick
off a small live one. Point out multiple cards sharing the same tag.

**VO:**
> "For a whole ad campaign, I write one brief and several variant prompts.
> Genblaze fans all of them out in one real orchestrated batch — not a
> loop calling generate four times — and every variant lands in B2 with
> its own manifest, grouped under one campaign ID."

### 6. Verify page — the trust check (1:50–2:20)

**Visual:** Navigate to `/verify`. Drag in the previously-downloaded
generated file → green "Authentic — provenance verified" result, showing
matched prompt/provider/date. Then drag in the unrelated random file →
"No provenance record found."

**VO:**
> "And this is the part anyone can use — no login. Drop in a file. If it
> was generated through Veritas, its content hash matches a manifest on
> B2, instantly — this is an O(1) index lookup, not a scan. Drop in
> anything else, or a modified copy of a real one, and it correctly comes
> back unverified. One changed pixel breaks the match."

### 7. Production-readiness proof — B2 + WORM (2:20–2:40)

**Visual:** Cut to the pre-opened B2 console tab showing the Object Lock
config on `veritas-genmedia-locked`. If you can, show a terminal attempt
to delete a locked object version returning `AccessDenied` (this is the
strongest, most judge-convincing shot in the whole video — a real
adversarial test, not a claim).

**VO:**
> "Every manifest also gets a write-once copy in a second, Object-Lock
> enabled B2 bucket, in compliance mode. I tested this like an attacker
> would — tried to delete a locked manifest directly. Access denied. Not
> even the account owner can quietly edit history."

### 8. Recap + close (2:40–2:55)

**Visual:** Cut back to the homepage stats row (Generated / Verified /
Campaigns), then a quick flash of the architecture: B2 as the only
datastore, Genblaze in the middle, providers on one side.

**VO:**
> "No separate database — B2 is the entire system of record: runs,
> manifests, lineage, the verify index, all of it. Built with Genblaze,
> Backblaze B2, and NVIDIA, deployed and live right now at the link
> below. This is Veritas — generate once, prove it forever."

**On-screen text, last 2 seconds:** `veritas-one-alpha.vercel.app`

| WORM proof | 20s | 2:40 |
| Recap + close | 15s | 2:55 |

That leaves 5s of slack under the 3:00 hard cap for a title card or
breathing room on cuts.

## What to cut first if you're running long

1. Iteration lineage (segment 4) — nice-to-have, not one of the four
   judging criteria directly.
2. The live "type and wait" generate moment — replace with pre-generated
   result and just narrate over the gallery + modal.
3. Campaign segment — shorten to a single sentence over the gallery view
   instead of a full walkthrough.

Do **not** cut the WORM/Object-Lock proof (segment 7) or the verify
mismatch demo (segment 6) — those are the two moments that make "trust
me, it's provenance-verified" into something a judge can actually see
falsified attempts fail against.
